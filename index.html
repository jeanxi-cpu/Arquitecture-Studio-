1/6 — Casa Meridiana

Casa Meridiana is positioned on a steep site in the northern savanna of Bogotá, 2,680 meters above sea level. The project responds to the specific climatic condition of this altitude: intense solar radiation during the day, significant temperature drops at night.

Rather than enclosing against the climate, the design embraces it — orienting the main living spaces to capture low winter sun while long roof overhangs protect against direct summer light. The structural logic is expressed in the material palette: raw concrete, local stone, and weathering steel form the primary envelope.
